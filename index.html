Fried Liver Attack

The Fried Liver Attack is a chess opening variation of the Italian Game, more specifically The Two Knights Defense.

Italian Game

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4

Two Knights Defense

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 Nf6

Fried Liver Attack

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc3 Nf6 4.Ng5 d5 5.exd5 Nxd5 6.Nxf7

Theory

            After 6...Kxf7, the following is usually played 7.Qf3+ Ke6 (7...Kg8?? mate in 3 after 8.Bxd5+ while other moves immediately 
            lose the knight on d5) 8.Nc3 (diagram). Black should play 8...Nb4 and follow up with ...c6, bolstering their pinned knight on 
            d5. White can force the b4-knight to stop protecting the d5-knight with 9.a3, but 
            Black is quite strong after 9.a3 Nxc2+ 10.Kd1 Nd4!?[3] or 10...Nxa1! 11.Nxd5 Qh4! when White's attack runs out (12.Nxc7+ 
            Kd7 13.Qf7+ Qe7 and the queens are traded; after 12.Nb6+ Ke7, 13.Nxc8+ is met by 13...Rxc8 14.d3 Kd8, and 13.Qf7+ is met 
            by 13...Kd8 14.Nxa8 Bd7; Black's king is safe and Black has the advantage).[7] Thus, 9.Bb3, 9.Qe4, or 9.0-0 are 
            better choices

Variations after 7.Qf3+

  • 7...Kg8
  • 7...Ke8
  • 7...Ke7
  • 7...Kg6
  • 7...Qf6
